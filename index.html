Proximity Classification

As you adjust the eye size, the tool checks whether the current ratio is near a mathematically notable value — a power of φ or Φ, a Fibonacci ratio, a harmonic fraction, or a geometric/transcendental constant. The match is labelled by how close it is:

Exact (within ~1 billionth)
The ratio is computationally indistinguishable from the known value at double-precision floating point. Whether arrived at by preset, equation, or slider, the math says it is that value. To ensure precision, use a formula in the Equation Input such as Phi/6 or phi^3.
Near-Exact (within ±0.1%)
Extremely close — well within any perceptible difference visually or in print. The relationship is almost certainly intentional, and the ratio could serve as a practical stand-in for the known value.
Adjacent (within ±0.5%)
Clearly related. The deviation is small enough that the proportions feel the same, though a careful measurement would reveal it. Useful as a near-substitute when the exact value is inconvenient.
Near (within ±2%)
A visible family resemblance. The ratio shares the character of the reference value — proportions will feel related but not identical under scrutiny.
Vicinal (within ±5%)
In the neighbourhood. The human eye may not distinguish this from the reference, but the difference is measurable. "Vicinal" is borrowed from crystallography, where it describes a face or ratio just off a principal plane, or "in the vicinity of."

Powers of Φ and φ are checked up to the 9th power. Fibonacci sequence ratios above F(3) naturally converge toward φ and Φ and will appear here. Other catalogued values include simple harmonic fractions (½, ⅓, ⅝ …), geometric constants (√2, √3, √5-based), and transcendental ratios (π and e based).
